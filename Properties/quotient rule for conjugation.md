The conjugate of a quotient equals the quotient of the conjugates, provided the denominator isn’t zero. In words: divide first and then take the conjugate, or take the conjugate of numerator and denominator and then divide—you get the same result. (Note: the standard identity has the conjugate on both numerator and denominator.)


$$
\overline{\left(\frac{z_1}{z_2}\right)}=\frac{\overline{z_1}}{\overline{z_2}} \quad\left(z_2 \neq 0\right) .
$$
