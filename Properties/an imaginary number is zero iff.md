
A complex number equals zero if and only if both its real part and its imaginary part are zero. So if $c+i d$ is not zero, then its conjugate $c-i d$ is also not zero, and this is equivalent to saying that the sum of the squares of $c$ and $d$ is not zero.


$$
c+i d \neq 0 \Leftrightarrow c-i d \neq 0 \Leftrightarrow c^2+d^2 \neq 0
$$
