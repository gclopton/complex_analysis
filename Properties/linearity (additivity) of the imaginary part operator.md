
Taking the imaginary part distributes across addition.


**Proof:** 
The sum of the complex numbers $z_1=x_1+i y_1, z_2=x_2+i y_2, \text { and } z_3=x_3+i y_3$ is just the combination of the sum of the real parts in one term, and the sum of the imaginary parts in the other term. So we have:


$$
z_1+z_2+z_3=\left(x_1+x_2+x_3\right)+i\left(y_1+y_2+y_3\right)
$$
 So $\operatorname{Im}(z_{1} + z_{2} + z_{3}) = y_{1} + y_{2} + y_{3}$. Now, note that


$$\operatorname{Im}z_{1}=y_1,\operatorname{Im}z_{2}=y_{2},\operatorname{Im}z_{3}=y_{3}$$


So $$\operatorname{Im}z_{1} + \operatorname{Im}z_{2} + \operatorname{Im}z_{3} = y_{1} + y_{2} + y{3}$$


**_Conclusions and Implications:_**
- Taking the imaginary part distributes across addition
- The map $z \mapsto \operatorname{Im}(z)$ is a real-linear functional on $\mathbb{C}$.
