If you take the conjugate of a complex number twice, you get back the original number.

**Proof:**
Let $z=a + ib$. Then $\bar{z} = a - ib$ and $\overline{\bar{z}}=\overline{a-i b}=a+i b=z$.

_**Conclusions and Implications**_
- Conjugation is its own inverse. That is, conjugation is an involutive automorphism of $\mathbb{C} \ \text{over} \ \mathbb{R}$.
