The conjugate of a sum equals the sum of the conjugates. In words: if you add two complex numbers and then take the conjugate, you get the same result as taking the conjugate of each number first and then adding. Similarly, the conjugate of a difference equals the difference of the conjugates. In words: subtract first and then conjugate, or conjugate first and then subtract—you get the same result.


$$
\overline{z_1+z_2}=\overline{z_1}+\overline{z_2}
$$
