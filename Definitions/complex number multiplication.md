
To multiply two complex numbers, treat them like binomials and use distribution: multiply the first number’s real part by the second’s real part, the real part by the imaginary part, the imaginary part by the real part, and finally the imaginary part by the imaginary part. Then combine everything. Remember that the imaginary unit squared equals negative one, so when you multiply the two imaginary parts together, that contributes a real number, not an imaginary one.


$$
\begin{aligned}
(a+i b)(c+i d) & =a c+i a d+i b c+i^2 b d \\
& =(a c-b d)+i(a d+b c)
\end{aligned}
$$


# Examples

## Example 1

$$
(-1+i)(2+i)=-2-i+2 i+i^2=-3+i
$$

## Example 2

$$
-i(4+4i) = -4 i-4(i)^2=-4 i+4=4-4 i
$$
