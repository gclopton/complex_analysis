
The conjugate of a complex number is the same number, but with the sign of the imaginary part flipped. The real part stays exactly the same, and the imaginary part changes from positive to negative or from negative to positive.


$$
\bar{z}=\overline{a+i b}=a-i b
$$

$$
\operatorname{Re} \bar{z}=\operatorname{Re} z \text { and } \operatorname{Im} \bar{z}=-\operatorname{Im} z .
$$
