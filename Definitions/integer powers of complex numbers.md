
To raise a complex number to an integer power, you multiply the number by itself as many times as the power says. For example, squaring a complex number means multiplying it by itself once. Cubing it means multiplying it by itself twice, and so on. Any nonzero complex number raised to the zero power equals one. For negative powers, you take the reciprocal.

$$
z^n=\overbrace{z \cdot z \cdots z}^{n \text { of these }}
$$

# Facts
- $z^{0}=1$
- $z^{-n} = \frac{1}{z^{n}}$
