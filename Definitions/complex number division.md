
To divide one complex number by another, you multiply the top and the bottom by the conjugate of the bottom. The top will be another complex number, and the bottom will be a single real number. Finish by splitting that fraction into a real part and an imaginary part.


$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{a+i b}{c+i d} & =\frac{(a+i b)(c-i d)}{(c+i d)(c-i d)} \\
& =\frac{(a c+b d)+(b c-a d) i}{c^2+d^2} \\
& =\frac{a c+b d}{c^2+d^2}+i \frac{b c-a d}{c^2+d^2}
\end{aligned}
$$
# Examples

## Example 1

$$
\frac{1}{i}=\frac{1}{i} \frac{-i}{-i}=-i
$$

## Example 2

If $z=x+i y \neq 0$ then

$$
\frac{1}{z}=\frac{x}{x^2+y^2}-i \frac{y}{x^2+y^2}
$$
